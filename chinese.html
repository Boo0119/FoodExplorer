Chinese Street Food

Chinese street food in Malaysia reflects a rich culinary heritage passed down through generations. With its bold flavors, wok traditions, and vibrant variety, it captures the spirit of family gatherings and lively markets.

Char Kway Teow is a famous stir-fried noodle dish from the Chinese (specifically Hokkien) community in Malaysia. It consists of flat rice noodles stir-fried with prawns, eggs, Chinese sausage, bean sprouts, and chives in a smoky, wok-fried style with a mix of soy sauces. The dish is often cooked with a touch of chili and served hot. [Translate]

Normal Price: RM 6.00 - RM 10.00

Popular In: Penang, Malaysia

Best Served With: Iced Lemon Tea

Ingredients:

  • Flat rice noodles
  • Prawns
  • Eggs
  • Chinese sausage
  • Bean sprouts
  • Chives
  • Lard
  • Chili (optional for spice)

Hokkien Mee is a hearty noodle dish that originated from the Hokkien Chinese community in Malaysia. The dish is usually served with a side of sambal chili for an extra kick. [Translate]

Popular In: Penang, Malaysia

Best Served With: Chilled Beer

Ingredients:

  • Yellow noodles
  • Prawns
  • Squid
  • Pork belly
  • Dark soy sauce
  • Water spinach
  • Garlic and chilis

Lok Lok is a popular street food that is a mix of Chinese and local influences, where a variety of ingredients like meat, vegetables, and seafood are skewered on sticks and then dipped into a boiling pot of soup or oil. Once cooked, it is dipped in a variety of sauces, such as peanut or chili sauce, for extra flavor. [Translate]

Normal Price: RM 1.00 - RM 3.00 per skewer

Popular In: Penang, Malaysia

Best Served With: Chili sauce

Ingredients:

  • Meat skewers (chicken, beef, fish)
  • Vegetables (cabbage, mushrooms, corn)
  • Rice cakes
  • Various dipping sauces
