Malay Street Food

Malay street food carries the warmth of community and tradition. It is colorful, aromatic, and deeply rooted in kampung culture, often bringing people together through its comforting and soulful taste.

Nasi Lemak 椰浆饭 ★★★★★ (5/5)

Nasi Lemak is often considered Malaysia's national dish, especially beloved by the Malay community. It consists of rice cooked in rich, creamy coconut milk, accompanied by sambal (spicy chili paste), fried anchovies, cucumber slices, and a hard-boiled egg. It can also be served with additional side dishes like chicken, beef rendang, or sambal sotong. [Translate]

Normal Price: RM 5.00 - RM 7.00

Popular In: Penang, Malaysia

Best Served With: Iced Tea

Ingredients:

  • Coconut rice
  • Sambal (spicy chili paste)
  • Anchovies (ikan bilis)
  • Hard-boiled egg
  • Cucumber slices

Satay 沙爹 ★★★★★ (5/5)

Satay is a popular grilled skewered meat dish that is marinated in a blend of spices and served with a rich peanut sauce. It is a dish that traces its roots back to the Malay community but has been widely embraced by various ethnic groups in Malaysia. Satay is typically made with chicken, beef, or lamb and is served with a side of rice cakes. [Translate]

Normal Price: RM 1.50 - RM 3.00 per stick

Popular In: Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia

Best Served With: Cucumber and Rice Cake

Ingredients:

  • Chicken, Beef, or Lamb
  • Peanut Sauce
  • Rice cakes
  • Cucumber

Roti Jala 网状面包 ★★★★☆ (4/5)

Roti Jala is a delicate Malay pancake-like dish made from a batter of flour, eggs, and coconut milk. It is cooked on a flat pan in a unique net-like shape, resembling a lace pattern. It is often served with rich curries like chicken curry or dal. [Translate]

Normal Price: RM 4.00 - RM 6.00

Popular In: Johor, Malaysia

Best Served With: Chicken Curry

Ingredients:

  • Flour
  • Eggs
  • Milk
  • Curry (for dipping)

Mee Goreng Mamak 炒面 ★★★★☆ (4/5)

Mee Goreng is a stir-fried noodle dish that is popular among Malaysians of all ethnic backgrounds. It is made with yellow noodles stir-fried with vegetables, eggs, tofu, and a choice of meat (chicken, beef, or seafood), all seasoned with soy sauce and chili for extra flavor. [Translate]

Normal Price: RM 5.00 - RM 8.00

Popular In: Penang, Malaysia

Best Served With: Lime and Chili Sauce

Ingredients:

  • Yellow noodles
  • Shrimp
  • Tofu
  • Eggs
  • Vegetables
  • Chili sauce (optional)

Roti Canai 印度煎饼 ★★★★☆ (4/5)

Roti Canai is a popular Indian-influenced flatbread that is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. It is made from a dough that is stretched and tossed until it becomes thin, then pan-fried. It is often served with dhal (lentil curry) or chicken curry. The Malay community has embraced this dish as a popular breakfast or tea-time snack. [Translate]

Normal Price: RM 2.00 - RM 3.50

Popular In: Northern Malaysia

Best Served With: Dhal or Sugar

Ingredients:

  • Flour
  • Butter or Ghee
  • Water
  • Salt

Nasi Kandar 印度米饭 ★★★★★ (5/5)

Nasi Kandar is a popular Malaysian dish of Indian Muslim origin (mamas). It features steamed rice served with a variety of curries and side dishes such as fried chicken, beef, or seafood. The rice is often drizzled with a mix of different gravies, creating a unique and flavorful meal.

Normal Price: RM 6.00 - RM 10.00

Popular In: Penang, Malaysia

Best Served With: Fried Chicken and Beef Curry

Ingredients:

  • Rice
  • Chicken curry
  • Beef rendang
  • Fried chicken
  • Vegetables
  • Lamb

Laksam 拉沙 ★★★★☆ (4/5)

Laksam is a traditional Malay noodle dish originating from the northern regions of Malaysia. It is made from thick rice noodles served in a rich, creamy coconut-based broth. The soup is flavored with herbs, and the dish is usually topped with fish, cucumber, and a drizzle of sambal for a bit of heat.

Normal Price: RM 6.00 - RM 8.00

Popular In: Kelantan & Terengganu, Malaysia

Best Served With: Sambal and Lemongrass

Ingredients:

  • Thick rice noodles
  • Fish (Mackerel or similar)
  • Coconut milk
  • Ginger and lemongrass
  • Sambal

Kuih Lapis 千层糕 ★★★★☆ (4/5)

Kuih Lapis is a traditional layered cake made from rice flour, sugar, coconut milk, and pandan juice. This colorful cake is a staple in Malay festivities and is known for its vibrant appearance and soft, chewy texture. [Translate]

Normal Price: RM 5.00 - RM 12.00

Popular In: Sarawak, Malaysia

Best Served With: Hot Tea

Ingredients:

  • Rice flour
  • Coconut milk
  • Sugar
  • Food coloring (for layers)

Mee Rebus 卤面 ★★★★☆ (4/5)

Mee Rebus is a noodle dish made with yellow noodles served in a thick, savory gravy made from potatoes, beans, and spices. Topped with boiled eggs, fried shallots, and a squeeze of lime, it is a comforting dish popular among the Malay community, particularly for breakfast or lunch. [Translate]

Normal Price: RM 5.00 - RM 8.00

Popular In: Johor, Malaysia

Best Served With: A dash of lime and chili paste

Ingredients:

  • Egg noodles
  • Spiced gravy (turmeric, tamarind, chili)
  • Boiled egg
  • Tofu
  • Lime

Roti John 约翰面包 ★★★★☆ (4/5)

Roti John is a popular sandwich originating from Malaysia, made with a baguette filled with minced meat (usually beef or chicken), eggs, onions, and a blend of spices. It is often served with ketchup and mayonnaise, and is a popular snack in the Malay community, especially at food stalls and street corners. [Translate]

Normal Price: RM 4.00 - RM 8.00

Popular In: Kuala Lumpur & Melaka, Malaysia

Best Served With: Chili sauce or mayonnaise

Ingredients:

  • French bread
  • Minced meat (beef or chicken)
  • Onions
  • Eggs
  • Chili sauce or mayonnaise
